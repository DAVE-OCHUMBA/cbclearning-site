4. Legal Basis for Processing

Under the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 (Section 30), we must have a valid legal basis for processing personal data. We rely on the following bases:

4.1 Contractual Necessity (Section 30(b))

Most processing on our platform is necessary to perform our contract with schools. When a school subscribes to CBC Learning Ecosystem, we must process student data to deliver CBC tracking, and parent data to deliver M-Pesa fee collection. Without this processing, we cannot provide the service.

4.2 Legitimate Interests (Section 30(e))

We process some data on the basis of legitimate interests — where our interests (or those of schools) do not override the rights of data subjects. This includes: security monitoring, fraud prevention, anonymised analytics, and maintaining audit logs. We always balance these interests against the rights of individuals.

4.3 Legal Obligation (Section 30(c))

We retain financial transaction records for 7 years as required by the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) and the Kenya Income Tax Act. We retain M-Pesa transaction records as required under Central Bank of Kenya regulations applicable to payment service facilitators.

4.4 Consent (Section 30(a))

Where we send marketing communications or share data for purposes beyond core service delivery, we obtain explicit consent. You may withdraw consent at any time by contacting dpo@cbclearning.co.ke. Withdrawal does not affect data processed before withdrawal.

5. Children's Data

Our platform processes personal data of students, many of whom are children under the age of 18. We take this responsibility seriously and comply with Section 45 of the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019, which establishes heightened requirements for processing children's personal data.

5.1 Lawful basis for processing children's data

Schools are the registered Data Controllers for their students' data under Kenyan law. By enrolling a student, schools accept responsibility for the student's data on behalf of parents/guardians, operating in loco parentis. CBC Learning Ecosystem processes student data strictly on the school's documented instructions.

5.2 What we do with children's data

  • We process student data only for school management purposes: CBC assessments, fee tracking, attendance, and MoE-mandated reporting.
  • We do not use children's data for advertising, profiling, or any commercial purpose beyond the school management service.
  • We do not share children's data with third parties except those listed in Section 6 of this policy.
  • We do not retain children's data beyond the retention periods specified in Section 8.

5.3 Parental access

Parents and guardians may request access to their child's data, request corrections, or request deletion by contacting dpo@cbclearning.co.ke. Requests will be fulfilled within 21 days, subject to the school's verification of parental relationship.

7. International Data Transfers

We are committed to keeping Kenyan personal data in Africa. Our data storage architecture:

  • Database: Hosted on Neon in the AWS Africa (Cape Town, South Africa) region. No data leaves Africa.
  • Application server: Hosted on Railway. Application processing occurs in a region geographically close to Kenya. No personal data is stored on Railway infrastructure — it is transmitted to the database.
  • SMS: Africa's Talking processes SMS delivery within their Africa-based infrastructure.

We do not transfer personal data to countries outside Africa without your explicit consent or a valid legal basis under Section 48 of the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019.

9. How We Protect Your Data

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, or destruction:

Technical measures

  • Encryption in transit: All data transmitted between your device and our servers is encrypted using TLS 1.3. HTTPS is enforced on all endpoints.
  • Encryption at rest: Our database is hosted on Neon (SOC 2 Type II certified) with AES-256 encryption at the storage layer.
  • Authentication: JWT-based authentication with 15-minute access token expiry and 7-day refresh token rotation. Passwords are hashed with bcrypt (never stored in plain text).
  • Access control: Role-based access ensures teachers can only see their own classes, and parents can only see their own children's data.
  • M-Pesa callback security: Our callback endpoints only accept connections from Safaricom's published IP ranges (196.201.214.0/24 and 196.201.216.0/24).
  • Audit logging: Every data access and modification is logged with timestamp, user ID, and IP address.

Organisational measures

  • Access to production systems is restricted to authorised personnel only.
  • Staff handling personal data are trained on data protection obligations.
  • We conduct security reviews before deploying new features that process personal data.

Data breach notification

In the event of a data breach that poses a risk to individuals' rights and freedoms, we will notify the ODPC within 72 hours of becoming aware, as required by Section 43 of the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019. We will notify affected individuals without undue delay where the breach poses a high risk to them.

To report a suspected security vulnerability or data breach, contact: dpo@cbclearning.co.ke

10. Your Rights

Under the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 (Sections 26–34), you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

👁️ Right of Access

You have the right to obtain a copy of all personal data we hold about you, free of charge. We will provide this in CSV format within 14 days of a verified request.

✏️ Right to Rectification

If any data we hold is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to have it corrected. We will action corrections within 5 business days of verification.

🗑️ Right to Erasure

You have the right to request deletion of your personal data. Where we have a legal obligation to retain data (e.g. financial records), we will anonymise rather than delete.

📦 Right to Data Portability

You can request your data in a portable, machine-readable format (JSON or CSV). Student CBC records are exportable at the school's request at any time.

🚫 Right to Object

Where we process data on the basis of legitimate interests, you may object. We will cease processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds.

🔒 Right to Restrict Processing

You may request that we restrict processing of your data while a complaint is being investigated or while you are exercising another right.

How to exercise your rights

Submit a data subject access request to: dpo@cbclearning.co.ke

We will acknowledge all requests within 3 business days and fulfil them within 21 days as required by the Act. We may need to verify your identity before processing the request.

Right to complain

If you believe we have handled your personal data unlawfully, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC):

  • Website: www.odpc.go.ke
